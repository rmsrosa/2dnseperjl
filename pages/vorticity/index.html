    1.2. Vorticity formulation

    An alternative formulation is with the vorticity. In the two-dimensional case, the vorticity is (essentially) a scalar, and the pressure disappears, making it easier to solve the system.

    When the velocity field is u=(u,v)\mathbf{u} = (u, v)u=(u,v) and the spatial variable is x=(x,y)\mathbf{x} = (x, y)x=(x,y), the (scalar) vorticity is given by

    ω=vx−uy, \omega = v_x - u_y, ω=vx​−uy​,

    where the subscripts denote the corresponding partial derivatives.

    Alternatively, if u=(u,v,0)\mathbf{u} = (u, v, 0)u=(u,v,0), then ω=∇×u=(0,0,ω)\mathbf{\omega} = \boldsymbol{\nabla} \times \mathbf{u} = (0, 0, \omega)ω=∇×u=(0,0,ω).

    By taking the curl of the linear momentum equation, we obtain

    ∂t(∇×u)+∇×((u⋅∇u)⋅u)+∇×∇p=νΔ(∇×u)+∇×f. \partial_t (\boldsymbol{\nabla} \times \mathbf{u}) + \boldsymbol{\nabla} \times ((\mathbf{u} \cdot \boldsymbol{\nabla} \mathbf{u})\cdot \mathbf{u}) + \boldsymbol{\nabla} \times \boldsymbol{\nabla} p = \nu \Delta (\boldsymbol{\nabla} \times \mathbf{u}) + \boldsymbol{\nabla} \times \mathbf{f}. ∂t​(∇×u)+∇×((u⋅∇u)⋅u)+∇×∇p=νΔ(∇×u)+∇×f.

    Notice that

    ∇×∇p=(0,0,0),∂t(∇×u)=(0,0,ωt),νΔ(∇×u)=(0,0,νΔω),∇×f=(0,0,g), \begin{align*} \displaystyle \boldsymbol{\nabla} \times \boldsymbol{\nabla} p & = (0, 0, 0), \\ \displaystyle \partial_t (\boldsymbol{\nabla} \times \mathbf{u}) & = (0, 0, \omega_t), \\ \displaystyle \nu \Delta (\boldsymbol{\nabla} \times \mathbf{u}) & = (0, 0, \nu\Delta\omega), \\ \displaystyle \boldsymbol{\nabla} \times \mathbf{f} & = (0, 0, g), \end{align*} ∇×∇p∂t​(∇×u)νΔ(∇×u)∇×f​=(0,0,0),=(0,0,ωt​),=(0,0,νΔω),=(0,0,g),​

    where g=(f1)y−(f2)xg = (f_1)_y - (f_2)_xg=(f1​)y​−(f2​)x​, where f1f_1f1​ and f2f_2f2​ are the two compenents of the force, f=(f1,f2)\mathbf{f} = (f_1, f_2)f=(f1​,f2​).

    As for the remaining term, we write

    ∇×((u⋅∇)u)=∇×(uux+vuyuvx+vvy0) \boldsymbol{\nabla} \times ((\mathbf{u}\cdot\boldsymbol{\nabla})\mathbf{u}) = \boldsymbol{\nabla} \times \left( \begin{matrix} uu_x + vu_y \\ uv_x + vv_y \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right) ∇×((u⋅∇)u)=∇×⎝⎛​uux​+vuy​uvx​+vvy​0​⎠⎞​

    The first two components of this curl vanish, while the last component is given by

    (uvx+vvy)x−(uux+vuy)y=uxvx+uvxx+vxvy+vvyx−uyux−uuxy−vyuy−vuyy=u(vxx−uxy)+v(vyx−uyy)+ux(vx−uy)+vy(vx−uy)=uωx+vωy+uxω+vyω=(uω)x+(vω)y=∇⋅(ωu). \begin{align*} (uv_x + vv_y)_x - (uu_x + vu_y)_y & = u_xv_x + uv_{xx} + v_xv_y + vv_{yx} - u_yu_x - uu_{xy} - v_yu_y -vu_{yy} \\ & = u(v_{xx} -u_{xy}) + v(v_{yx}-u_{yy}) + u_x(v_x-u_y) + v_y(v_x-u_y) \\ & = u\omega_x + v\omega_y + u_x\omega + v_y\omega = (u\omega)_x + (v\omega)y = \boldsymbol{\nabla}\cdot(\omega\mathbf{u}). \end{align*} (uvx​+vvy​)x​−(uux​+vuy​)y​​=ux​vx​+uvxx​+vx​vy​+vvyx​−uy​ux​−uuxy​−vy​uy​−vuyy​=u(vxx​−uxy​)+v(vyx​−uyy​)+ux​(vx​−uy​)+vy​(vx​−uy​)=uωx​+vωy​+ux​ω+vy​ω=(uω)x​+(vω)y=∇⋅(ωu).​

    Combining the expressions and considereing only the zzz-component, we find the two-dimensional vorticity equation

    ωt+∇⋅(ωu)=νΔω+g. \omega_t + \boldsymbol{\nabla}\cdot(\omega\mathbf{u}) = \nu\Delta \omega + g. ωt​+∇⋅(ωu)=νΔω+g.

    Due to the divergence-free condition on the velocity field, we have

    ∇⋅(ωu)=∇ω⋅u+ω∇⋅u=∇ω⋅u. \boldsymbol{\nabla}\cdot(\omega\mathbf{u}) = \boldsymbol{\nabla}\omega \cdot \mathbf{u} + \omega \boldsymbol{\nabla}\cdot \mathbf{u} = \boldsymbol{\nabla}\omega \cdot \mathbf{u}. ∇⋅(ωu)=∇ω⋅u+ω∇⋅u=∇ω⋅u.

    But we keep the form ∇⋅(ωu),\boldsymbol{\nabla}\cdot(\omega\mathbf{u}),∇⋅(ωu), in order to apply the Basdevant reduction, which requires less Fourier transforms; see 1.3. Basdevant reduction.
